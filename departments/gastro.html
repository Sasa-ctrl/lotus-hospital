About Us

The Gastroenterology department at Lotus Hospital is a leading center that integrates advanced medical technology with a team of highly skilled specialists to provide comprehensive care for digestive and liver disorders. Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)

GERD is a chronic condition where stomach acid flows back into the esophagus, causing heartburn, chest pain, and difficulty swallowing. It is common due to spicy food consumption, obesity, and irregular eating habits. Long-term GERD can lead to complications like esophagitis and ulcers. Lifestyle changes, avoiding acidic foods, and medications like antacids and proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) help in management. Severe cases may require surgery. Proper diet and weight management can prevent GERD.

Peptic ulcers are painful sores that develop in the stomach or small intestine lining, often caused by H. pylori infection or excessive NSAID (painkiller) use. Symptoms include burning stomach pain, bloating, nausea, and vomiting. Spicy food, smoking, and alcohol worsen the condition. Diagnosis is done through endoscopy and H. pylori tests. Treatment includes acid-reducing medications, antibiotics, and lifestyle modifications. Untreated ulcers can lead to internal bleeding or perforation.

IBS is a functional digestive disorder affecting the large intestine, leading to abdominal pain, bloating, gas, diarrhea, or constipation. It is often triggered by stress, poor diet, and infections. Though not life-threatening, it significantly impacts daily life. Diagnosis is based on symptoms as no specific test confirms IBS. Management includes dietary changes (low-FODMAP diet), stress management, probiotics, and medications. Regular exercise and hydration help in symptom relief.
