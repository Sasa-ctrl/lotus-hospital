Otitis Media (Middle Ear Infection)
Chronic Sinusitis
Tonsillitis

Otitis media is an infection of the middle ear, common among children, caused by bacterial or viral infections. It leads to ear pain, fever, hearing difficulty, and fluid buildup behind the eardrum. The condition can result from respiratory infections spreading to the ear through the Eustachian tube. Poor hygiene, malnutrition, and frequent colds make it widespread in India. Treatment includes pain relievers, antibiotics, and in chronic cases, surgical drainage.

Chronic sinusitis is a long-term inflammation of the sinuses, causing nasal congestion, facial pain, and a persistent runny nose. It is often triggered by allergies, pollution, or repeated infections. The condition leads to mucus buildup, creating an environment for bacterial growth. Symptoms include headache, pressure around the eyes, and postnasal drip. Treatment involves nasal sprays, antibiotics (if bacterial), and in severe cases, surgery. Poor air quality and high pollution levels contribute to its prevalence in India.

Tonsillitis is the inflammation of the tonsils due to viral or bacterial infections, leading to sore throat, difficulty swallowing, fever, and swollen lymph nodes. Streptococcus bacteria are the most common cause, but viral infections are also prevalent. It spreads through respiratory droplets, making it highly contagious, especially among school children. Recurrent cases may require tonsillectomy (surgical removal of tonsils). Proper hygiene, hydration, and antibiotics (for bacterial infections) help in management.
