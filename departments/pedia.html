Pneumonia is a serious respiratory infection causing inflammation in the lungs, leading to difficulty in breathing. It is a leading cause of death among children under five in India, often linked to malnutrition and air pollution. Symptoms include fever, cough, chest pain, and rapid breathing. Bacterial pneumonia (caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae) is treatable with antibiotics, while viral pneumonia requires supportive care. Early vaccination (PCV and Hib vaccine) and improved nutrition help in prevention. Delayed treatment can lead to severe complications like respiratory failure.

Diarrheal diseases, caused by bacterial, viral, or parasitic infections, are a leading cause of child mortality in India. Contaminated food, water, and poor sanitation contribute to frequent outbreaks. Symptoms include loose stools, dehydration, vomiting, and fever. Severe dehydration can be life-threatening if not treated promptly. Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS) and zinc supplements are the primary treatments. Improved hygiene, safe drinking water, and vaccination (like the rotavirus vaccine) help in prevention.

Malnutrition is a major health issue in Indian children, leading to stunted growth, weakened immunity, and developmental delays. It results from inadequate nutrition, poverty, poor maternal health, and infections. Symptoms include low weight, thin limbs, swollen belly (in severe cases), and frequent illnesses. Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM) requires urgent medical intervention with therapeutic foods. Government programs like Mid-Day Meal and ICDS aim to combat malnutrition. Balanced nutrition, breastfeeding, and supplementation (iron, vitamin A) are essential for prevention.
