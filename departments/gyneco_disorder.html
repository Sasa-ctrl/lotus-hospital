About Us

The Gynecology department at Lotus Hospital is a state-of-the-art facility that integrates advanced medical technology with a team of experienced specialists to provide comprehensive care for a wide range of gynecological conditions. Equipped with cutting-edge technologies such as high-definition laparoscopy, robotic-assisted surgery, 3D/4D ultrasound, and advanced hysteroscopy systems, we ensure precise diagnosis and effective treatment. It is one of the most trusted referral centers in India, specializing in the management of complex gynecological disorders, including endometriosis, fibroids, ovarian cysts, polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS), uterine prolapse, and gynecological cancers. Our team is committed to providing personalized, compassionate care tailored to every woman's unique health needs.

PCOS is a hormonal disorder that affects women of reproductive age, leading to irregular periods, excessive hair growth, acne, and weight gain. It occurs due to hormonal imbalances and insulin resistance, causing the ovaries to develop multiple small cysts. PCOS increases the risk of infertility, diabetes, and heart disease. Treatment includes lifestyle changes, weight management, hormonal therapy, and medications like metformin. Early diagnosis helps manage symptoms effectively.

Endometriosis is a condition where tissue similar to the uterine lining grows outside the uterus, leading to severe pelvic pain, painful periods, and infertility. The cause is unclear, but factors like genetics and hormonal imbalances may contribute. It can affect the ovaries, fallopian tubes, and pelvic tissues. Diagnosis is done through ultrasound or laparoscopy. Treatment includes pain management, hormonal therapy, and in severe cases, surgery. Early treatment can prevent complications.

Uterine fibroids are non-cancerous growths in the uterus that can cause heavy menstrual bleeding, pelvic pain, frequent urination, and infertility. They vary in size and can grow inside or outside the uterus. The exact cause is unknown, but hormonal changes and genetic factors play a role. Diagnosis is done through ultrasound or MRI. Treatment options include medications, hormonal therapy, or surgical removal (myomectomy or hysterectomy). Many fibroids are harmless and require only monitoring.
