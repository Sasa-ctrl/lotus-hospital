About Us

The Spine Surgery department at Lotus Hospital is a center of excellence, integrating advanced technology with highly skilled specialists to offer comprehensive care for spinal disorders and injuries. Equipped with cutting-edge technologies such as minimally invasive spine surgery, intraoperative neuro-navigation, robotic-assisted spine surgery, and advanced imaging techniques, the department ensures precision-driven treatments for various spinal conditions. It is one of the most trusted referral centers in India, specializing in complex procedures such as spinal decompression, disc replacement, scoliosis correction, spinal fusion, and endoscopic spine surgery. With a focus on patient-centric care and rehabilitation, the department provides world-class treatment for both degenerative and trauma-related spinal conditions.

A slipped or herniated disc occurs when the soft cushion between the vertebrae bulges out, pressing on nearby nerves. It commonly affects the lower back (lumbar spine) or neck (cervical spine). Symptoms include severe back pain, numbness, tingling, and weakness in limbs. It is often caused by poor posture, heavy lifting, obesity, or aging. Treatment includes physiotherapy, pain relief medications, and lifestyle modifications. Severe cases may require surgery (discectomy or spinal fusion).

Spondylosis is an age-related degeneration of the spine, leading to pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility. It is commonly seen in the cervical (neck) and lumbar (lower back) spine. Factors like sedentary lifestyle, poor posture, obesity, and genetic predisposition contribute to this condition. Symptoms include chronic pain, tingling sensations, and difficulty in movement. Treatment focuses on pain management, physiotherapy, and posture correction. In severe cases, spinal surgery may be required.

Sciatica is a condition where the sciatic nerve (running from the lower back to the legs) gets compressed, causing sharp pain, tingling, or numbness. It is often caused by herniated discs, spinal stenosis, or piriformis syndrome. Symptoms include radiating pain from the lower back to the legs, difficulty walking, and weakness in the affected leg. Treatment includes physiotherapy, pain relievers, stretching exercises, and posture correction. Severe cases may require nerve decompression surgery.
